# Channel Playbook — B2C

Per-channel notes for the currently active portfolio. Update a channel's section as real
results come in — right now these are strategic starting points, not proven playbooks.

## Instagram (primary organic engine)

- **Identity: a humor account about the universal humiliation of talking to other humans —
  not a tips account.** This is the single most important constraint on this channel. Every
  piece should pass the test: "would this work on a general humor/relatable-pain account, or
  does it only work because it's teaching something?" If it's the latter, it's off-brand
  for this channel.
- Roughly 60% pain memes, no CTA. The rest can carry a lighter touch of product connection,
  but resist making every post a pitch.
- Reels > static posts for reach; use the content agent tool's pillar/situation-arena system
  to keep ideas mapped to real freeze-up moments instead of drifting generic.
- Comment-to-DM (ManyChat) is a planned conversion mechanic layered on top of reels — the
  content itself should still work as pure entertainment even without the DM flow attached.

## YouTube Shorts (primary organic engine, paired with IG)

- Same content identity as Instagram — don't fork a different, more "educational" voice for
  YouTube just because the platform has historically rewarded tips content. Consistency of
  identity across the two primary channels matters more than platform-native optimization
  right now.
- Shorts can tolerate slightly longer setup than IG reels before the punchline/pain-moment
  lands — use that room, don't pad with filler.

## TikTok — deprioritized

- Blocked on having a US-based poster; don't propose a content calendar here until that's
  resolved. If asked to plan for TikTok anyway, say so directly rather than quietly building
  a plan around a blocker that hasn't been addressed.

## Product Hunt / Show HN / BetaList / Peerlist

- These are launch/visibility moments, not ongoing content channels — treat each as a single
  high-effort push (assets, timing, comment engagement plan) rather than a drip content
  strategy.
- Show HN audience skews technical/builder — positioning can lean into the product/AI
  mechanics more than it would on IG. Product Hunt audience is broader early-adopter; keep
  the pain-point framing front and center there too.
- Peerlist and BetaList skew toward founders/indie-hackers — a founder-to-founder tone
  ("built this because I kept freezing in meetings myself") can outperform a polished pitch.

## Creator affiliate DMs (revenue-share)

- This is direct outreach, not broadcast content — treat each DM as a real pitch to a real
  person, not a copy-pasted template. Direct outreach and high-value offers are the stated
  higher-converting lever vs. random platform posting — protect that by keeping outreach
  genuinely personalized, not templated at scale.
- The offer needs to be concrete before outreach starts: revenue-share terms, what the
  creator gets, what's expected of them. Don't send vague "let's collab" messages.

## ESL teacher / career coach / university career center outreach

- Different pain framing than the core consumer angle: these are potential referral partners
  who work with people who have the freeze-up problem, not necessarily people who have it
  themselves. Pitch the outcome for *their* clients/students, not the same meme-style hook
  used on Instagram.
- These outreach angles should probably explicitly distinguish Locuta from ESL/language
  products, since the audience is used to thinking in ESL-tool terms — make the "you already
  speak English, this isn't that" distinction early and clearly.

## AI tool directories

- Low-effort, high-volume — fine to batch, but the listing copy still needs the specific
  angle (freeze-up moments), not a generic "AI communication coach" description that reads
  identically to every other listing on the page.

## Community seeding (Facebook groups, Discord)

- Match the community's actual norms — a group for job seekers, a group for public speaking
  anxiety, and a general productivity Discord all warrant different framing of the same core
  product. Don't post one canned message across all of them.
- Genuine participation before promotion — seeding that reads as drive-by self-promo burns
  the channel fast in small communities.

## Paid channels — off the table

Don't propose paid acquisition (ads on any platform) as a lever under the current GTM plan.
If the economics or constraints genuinely change, flag that as a deliberate strategy
conversation to have explicitly — not something to slip into a recommendation by default.

## Reddit — excluded

Don't suggest Reddit as a channel. If asked directly why, the honest answer is this was a
deliberate exclusion in the current strategy — don't invent a rationale, just note it's
off the list.

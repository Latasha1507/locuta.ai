# Content Review & Planning

Two jobs live here: **reviewing** content (Latasha's drafts or your own before it ships) and
**planning** a content cadence across channels. Both fall under "marketing head," not just
writing individual pieces.

## Reviewing a draft (yours or Latasha's)

Don't just polish grammar — review for strategy first, craft second:

1. **What's the angle, and is it the sharpest available one?** If three ideas are competing
   in one piece, name that and say which one to keep.
2. **Does it target the real pain** (freezing up in a language you already speak) or has it
   drifted toward generic "improve your English" territory? This is the single most common
   way content quietly goes off-brand — flag it plainly when it happens.
3. **Does it match the channel it's headed for?** A line built for an Instagram meme caption
   and a line built for a Show HN post should not read the same. Check against
   `channel-playbook.md`.
4. **Is the CTA (or its absence) a deliberate choice?** Per the 60%-no-CTA content style, a
   CTA shouldn't be reflexive.
5. **Would you personally stop scrolling for this, honestly?** If the answer requires
   generosity, it's not ready.
6. **Is the tone raw and conversational, or does it read agency-polished?** Over-produced
   copy undercuts the whole positioning for this audience — flag it even if it's
   "well-written" by conventional standards.

Give the review as a straight verdict plus reasoning — "this works because X" or "cut the
CTA, weakens it" — not a hedge-everything list of minor tweaks that avoids saying whether the
piece is actually good.

## Planning a content cadence

When asked to plan out content (a week, a launch push, a batch of reels):

- **Start from pillars/situation-arenas, not a content calendar template.** The existing
  content agent tool organizes ideas this way — use that structure (freeze-up moments by
  situation: meetings, interviews, small talk, presentations, Slack/email hesitation, etc.)
  rather than generic "Monday: motivation, Wednesday: tips" calendar thinking.
- **Keep the pain-to-tips ratio intentional.** Roughly 60% pain/meme content with no CTA,
  the rest can carry lighter product connection. When planning a batch, check the ratio
  explicitly rather than letting it drift toward more product-forward content because that
  feels more "productive."
- **Batch by channel, not by day**, since Instagram and YouTube Shorts share an identity but
  Product Hunt/Show HN/directory listings are one-off pushes, not a drip cadence — don't force
  everything into one calendar structure.
- **Flag repetition.** If a batch of ideas all lean on the same 1-2 situations (e.g. every
  idea is about meetings), say so — the pillar system exists specifically to avoid this.
- **Name what you're unsure will land.** Not every idea in a batch needs to be presented with
  equal confidence — flag the safer bets vs. the ones worth testing.

## When reviewing performance (once real data exists)

- Distinguish reach/engagement from actual conversion — a viral piece that doesn't move
  signups and a quiet piece that drives high-intent traffic are different kinds of wins;
  don't conflate them in a report.
- When something clearly worked or flopped, write the "why" into
  `SKILL.md → PROJECT-SPECIFIC NOTES`, not just this file — that's where it'll actually get
  read again next time content gets planned.
